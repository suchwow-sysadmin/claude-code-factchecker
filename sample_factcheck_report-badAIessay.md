## Content Quality Issues

### Potential Factual Confusion

#### ⚠️ Historical Accuracy Concern (Line 9):

- Essay states Cavendish was "rescued from near-extinction several decades ago"
- Issue: This is historically backwards
- Actual history: The Gros Michel banana went functionally extinct commercially
  due to Panama disease (Fusarium oxysporum f.sp. cubense Race 1) in the
  1950s-60s and was replaced by the Cavendish, which was resistant to that
  strain
- Current threat: Cavendish is NOW threatened by TR4 (Tropical Race 4) of Panama
  disease
- Action: Clarify historical sequence and cite banana industry history sources

### TR4 vs. Climate Change

#### ⚠️ Conflated Threats (Line 21-22):

- Essay claims extinction "due to climate change-driven fungal blight, pests,
  and other invasive species"
- Issue: Primary threat to Cavendish is TR4 (Tropical Race 4) Panama disease, a
  fungal pathogen
- Climate connection: Climate change may facilitate spread, but TR4 is the
  direct threat
- Action: Separate and clarify: a. Primary threat: TR4 fungal disease b.
  Contributing factors: Climate change impacts on disease spread c. Secondary
  threats: Other pests/diseases
- Required citation: Research on TR4 and its global spread
